Key Facts — Retail Presence
Unbranded / Loose Mitti: Still sold by weight in thousands of mandi stalls and kirana stores, especially in tier-2 and rural India. Typically ₹10–30 per 100g.

Chemist / Ayurveda: Most chemist shops stock 2–4 Multani Mitti brands (Patanjali, Khadi, local brand), especially in North and West India.

How the Entire Business System Works

The Multani Mitti supply chain has 6 levels — from mine owner to end customer. Each level adds value and margin. Understanding which level you enter at determines your investment, risk, and profit.

Common Business Models
Home Packing Unit (₹30K+): Buy raw mitti from Barmer supplier, pack at home in branded pouches. 300 packets/day × ₹26.6 margin = ₹8,000/day profit. Very low risk.
Online D2C Brand (₹2–5L): Brand + Amazon/Nykaa/Flipkart seller account. Focus on organic, Ayurvedic positioning. Many small brands do ₹5–15L/month revenue.
Wholesale Supply to Salons (₹2–4L): Buy in bulk, supply 200–500g pouches to local parlours. Recurring B2B relationship. Very stable income stream.
B2B Manufacturer (₹15–50L): Set up processing unit, supply to cosmetics brands as private label. Long-term contracts. Scalable and export-ready.
Export House (₹50L+): ISO/GMP certified, IEC code, Alibaba listing, international courier/freight tie-up. Supply to Indian diaspora worldwide.

Export Channel — How It Works
Step 1 — Get IEC Code: Import Export Code from DGFT. Mandatory for all Indian exporters. Takes 1–2 business days online.
Step 2 — Register on B2B Portals: Alibaba, IndiaMART Global, ExportersIndia.com. Upload product photos, pricing, MOQ.
Step 3 — Amazon Global Selling: Companies like MiNature (Ahmedabad) started with B2B Multani Mitti supply, then launched own brand exporting to 6 countries with 20,000 customers/month.
Step 4 — Freight Forwarder: Tie up with air/sea freight agent. Most small shipments go via DHL/FedEx for quick commerce. Large orders via FCL/LCL containers.
Step 5 — Payment: SWIFT bank transfer, PayPal, or Payoneer. Letter of Credit (LC) for large institutional buyers.

SWOT Analysis + Future Growth Outlook

💪 Strengths
· India controls 91%+ of global exports
· 256.7 million tonne domestic reserves
· Mineral mined at just ₹4–8/kg
· Centuries of consumer trust & recognition
· Multi-sector demand: beauty, pharma, industrial
· Very low entry cost for entrepreneurs
⚠️ Weaknesses
· Mostly unorganised sector, poor quality control
· No GI tag or single quality standard
· Low global brand recognition for Indian labels
· Production concentrated in single belt (Barmer)
· Adulteration common in unbranded segment
· R&D investment is minimal across industry
